Dress appropriately for the temple.

It is preferred you wear traditional Indian clothing to the temple.  Indian attire is best suited for the several activities we exercise during our visit to the temple such as, the Shashtangam namaskarams (prostrations on the ground), Padmasanam (sitting cross-legged),  Pradakshanams (Circumambulation of the temple) etc.
  • Please avoid wearing shorts, short-skirts, torn-jeans, or any clothing's that would make temple activities (as listed above) uncomfortable or unsuitable.
  • Please note that hats, caps, etc are not allowed while on temple premises, especially within the temple main halls
  • Indian attire, especially for volunteering in specific religious activities is a must.  Please reach out to Temple Management for any clarifications on specific religious events'/activities wear.

Food Offerings
No cooked food from outside is allowed or permitted on the temple premises.  Devotees can bring offerings of fresh fruits, and nuts such as almonds, cashews, walnuts that are in a sealed bag.  Priests will offer that during the puja rituals. But please note no cooked food from outside is allowed or permitted on the temple premises.
